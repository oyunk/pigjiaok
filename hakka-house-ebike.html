Why Salt is the Future of Sustainable Tech

Sodium, a key part of table salt, is the sixth most common element on Earth, and we can get it right from seawater or rocks underground !

This makes rechargeable sodium batteries much cheaper and easier to make than lithium ones.

They are also incredibly stable. They don't easily overheat and handle freezing temperatures way better than standard batteries (lithium).

Because lithium is a much smaller atom, its electric charge is tightly packed into a tiny space. In contrast, sodium's charge is spread out over a much larger ion. This makes it chemically calmer and far less likely to trigger an explosive reaction !


Shenzhen: the Tech Hub Born from Salt

China pioneered the world's first sodium-powered electric vehicles (EVs), light scooters, and massive grid-scale power storage stations !

This massive push into next-generation energy has made Shenzhen a key part of China's high-tech revolution.

Yet long before Shenzhen became a global technology hub, the city relied on its coastal location (next to Hong Kong) and a booming salt trade to drive its growth for hundreds of years !
