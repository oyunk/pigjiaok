How is carbonated water💨💧 produced?

Factories make fizzy water by chilling it and pumping in carbon dioxide gas under high pressure. Sealing the bottle traps the gas inside the liquid.

While sealed, some gas💨 dissolve with the water💧 to create a mild acid called carbonic acid💨💧 : CO2+H2O⇌H2CO3CO. This safe acid gives the drink its sharp, tangy taste😋!

Opening the cap instantly drops the pressure inside the bottle. This breaks the chemical balance and causes a reaction called effervescence.

The acid turns back into carbon dioxide gas and rushes out as the 🫧bubbles🫧 you see rising. Because the gas inside the bubbles is much lighter and less dense than water, they quickly float up⬆️ to the surface!
