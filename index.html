How does the drone navigate the sky? ☁️☁️🧭

Cloud technology is like a super-powered brain in the sky that makes drone delivery work.

Drones are too small to carry heavy, powerful computers. If they tried, their batteries would drain way too fast !

Instead, drones send their hardest thinking tasks up to the cloud. Here is how the cloud helps them fly safely and automatically :
  • Smart Routing : It calculates the fastest paths while adjusting for strong winds !

  • Instant Changes : Drones get live updates to steer around unexpected obstacles or banned flying zones. For example :

    • If a temporary crane is built over a weekend, the cloud system updates the maps instantly so drones don't crash into it

    • If a storm breaks a large branch, the drone uses its smart cameras to see it and fly around

  • Team Control : It tracks entire fleets of drones at the same time so they can talk to each other and share flight data. This helps them fly together safely, coordinate landing spots, and avoid crashing into one another

  • Weather Watching : The cloud monitors storms so drones do not get caught in bad weather

Stone Boar Water filter

The head's ears function as air relief vents. They contain screens to keep insects out.

Moving down, the mouth acts as the overflow pipe with a louvered outlet that prevents pests from entering. The excess water is diverted into the mud basin.

The remaining water flows to the bottom of the sediment filter, where it is channelled directly into the solar still basin.

In this basin, the water undergoes natural purification as it evaporates and condenses against the cool glass surface !

Pig Dragon
(zhūlóng 猪龙) The Pig Dragon (zhūlóng 猪龙) - head of a pig and coiled, stout body of a dragon - is widely considered the first representation of the Chinese dragon !

Pigs were among the earliest animals domesticated in China. They have been found in graves buried alongside humans in Neolithic China.

The Pig Dragon eventually gave way to the familiar elongated form of the later Chinese lóng 龙 (dragon) !
